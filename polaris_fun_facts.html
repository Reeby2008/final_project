Polaris Fun Facts

  1. Polaris is eight times the mass of our sun and 2,000 times brighter.

  2. Polaris has been a visual aid to astronomers and navigators for centuries.

  3. Sailors on sea used to measure the angle of the star from the horizon, and would identify their latitudes with the help of Polaris.

  4. Polaris’ companion stars (Polaris AB and Polaris B) are both yellow-white dwarfs.

  5. Polaris is the only star that lies motionless in the sky.

  6. Polaris is the 48th brightest star, but doesn’t look that bright to us because it is so far away.

  7. Polaris has a surface temperature of 7,200K.

  8. Polaris is considered to be the end of a spike which the sky rotates around, in Norse tales.

  9. In Greek mythology, Polaris is connected to the story of Callisto, who was transformed into the constellation Ursa Major by Zeus.

  10. In astrology, Polaris is thought of as an individual’s “north node” of destiny, which is a path that leads to more fulfillment and purpose.
